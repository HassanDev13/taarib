Tesseract vs Google Cloud Vision vs Gemini 1.5 Flash-Lite

Executive Summary & Cost Analysis

We have evolved our term extraction architecture through three phases: local open-source OCR (Tesseract), cloud API OCR (Google Vision), and finally Multimodal AI (Gemini Flash-Lite).

Architecture Methodology Cost (per 1,000 pages) Verdict
Tesseract + DeepSeek Local OCR parses text -> DeepSeek LLM structures it. ~$0.15 (LLM input only) Highly inaccurate due to poor OCR parsing of Arabic columns.
Google Vision + DeepSeek Cloud OCR parses text -> DeepSeek LLM structures it. ~$1.65 ($1.50 Vision + $0.15 LLM) Expensive, high latency, double failure points.
Gemini Flash-Lite Multimodal: Image straight to Gemini for native JSON parsing. ~$0.10 (Image tokens only) 16x cheaper, natively understands layout, near-perfect accuracy. Recommended.

To truly compare the accuracy of these engines, we look at: Total Extraction Volume, Percentage of Perfect (9-10) Confidence Matches, and Arabic Character Length (Older OCR models drop words, while Gemini captures full compound explanations).

Gemini Flash-Lite Findings

During the latest database query, Gemini Flash-Lite successfully extracted 469 unique terms. Unlike text-based OCR models that chop words when they encounter large spaces, Gemini parses the image visually. This resulted in an average Arabic explanation length of 19.5 characters (almost double the previous models), proving it is extracting the full term definitions instead of just partial strings. The average confidence score assigned was 9.75 out of 10.
